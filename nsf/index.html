Deforestation

Cutting down forests to create farms or pastures, or for other reasons, causes emissions, since trees, when they are cut, release the carbon they have been storing.
Since forests absorb carbon dioxide, destroying them also limits nature’s ability to keep emissions out of the atmosphere.

Amazon Rainforest

With an estimated 16,000 species of trees, the Amazon Rainforest is teeming with natural resources.
The graph above visualises the annual volume of deforestation in the Amazon Rainforest (km²) from 2004 to 2019. You may filter the data by Brazilian state and mouse over a bar to see the exact deforestation volume.

Our Mission

Why are we so passionate about the environment? Simply put, the Earth's natural resources are being consumed at a rate that is unsustainable for future generations.
Our goal is to raise public awareness for sustainable living and contribute funds to conservation efforts.

Global Warming: Greenhouse Gas Emissions

The long-term heating of Earth's climate that began in the pre-industrial period due to human activity. The main driver is fossil fuel burning as it increases heat-trapping greenhouse gases into our atmosphere.

This graph shows the greenhouse gas emissions of the several distinct regions/continents on Earth from 1965 to 2020 by million metric tons of carbon dioxide.

For a deeper comparison between emission levels, click on any of the continents in the legend to hide them from the graph.
