Fossil Fuels

Generating electricity and heat by burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil and natural gas releases a large amount of greenhouse gases, trapping heat in our atmosphere
Most electricity is still produced from fossil fuels; only about a quarter comes from wind, solar and other renewable sources.

View Data

Food Production

Producing food requires energy to run farm equipment or fishing boats, usually with fossil fuels. Growing crops can also cause emissions, like when using fertilisers and manure.
Cattle produce methane, a powerful greenhouse gas. Emissions also come from packaging and distributing food.

View Data

Global Warming

The long-term heating of Earth's climate that began in the pre-industrial period due to human activity. The main driver is fossil fuel burning as it increases heat-trapping greenhouse gases into our atmosphere.
